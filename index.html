Author:

Torjei Helset

Supervisors:

This is a repository containing the code associated with my master's thesis. The master's thesis builds upon the work done for a semester thesis. The semester thesis can be freely downloaded from Semester Thesis, and the master's thesis can be freely downloaded from Master's Thesis. This homepage contains some animations showing some of the results. For all the examples considered, the densities on each road is represented by a red color scale. A white color means that there is no traffic, and a darker red color means that there is heavy traffic. The exmples compare how efficiently buses are able to drive through different traffic-flow networks for different choices of control parameters. In all cases we try to minimize the average delay of the buses. The traffic shown in the network as well is the traffic from the simulation when optimal control parameters was chosen. The green squares show the positions of the buses for optimal control parameters. In order to compare this with the non-optimal control parameters the optimizaiton algorithm was started from we also show the corresponding positions of the buses at the same times when non-optimal control paramters was chosen. These buses are visualized by the blue squares. These are added just to compare the different choices. We do not show the corresponding traffic on the roads for non-optimal control parameters. We also show the locations of the bus stops by a bus stop icon. We note that the positions of these icons might not match perfectly with the actual locations of the stops.

Bus Stopping at Bus Stop

Before looking at full traffic-flow networks, we look at only how the bus slows down coming into a bus stop for some different cases. The animation below shows four different cases. The topmost road is a single unidirectional road. Here, there is little space for other drivers to pass the bus, and so there is a high amount of traffic buildup behind the bus. The second road is a model of a four lane road. Since there are four different lanes, the traffic is only impacted to a small degree, and so there is little buildup of traffic behind the bus. In the third case we model to single lane unidirectional roads going in opposite direction. The opposing lane has little traffic, and so drivers can use also this road to overtake the bus, resulting in less buildup of traffic. In the final example, we again consider two lanes going in opposite directions. In this case however, there is a higher amount of traffic on the opposing lane, resulting in more buildup of traffic.

Single Lane Network

The first traffic-flow network we consider is a network consisting of only one road. The starting speed limit was chosen as 20 km/h, and the best speed limit found was 50 km/h.

Single Junction Network

The second example we consider is a traffic-flow network consisting of two roads connected in a junction. The traffic across this junction is controlled by a traffic light. In this case we need to determine good speed limits, as well as good settings for the traffic lights. A comparison of the initial guess for the control parameters and the best choice of control parameters can be seen in the video below.

Two-to-two Junction Network

We now consider a traffic-flow network consisting of four roads connected in a 2-to-2 junction. The traffic crossing the junction is controlled by a traffic light. In this example we consider two different bus lines.

Larger Network

We increase the complexity by adding a roundabout, as well as more roads and junctions. We also increase the number of bus lines adding a thirds bus line. The bottom left junction is controlled by a traffic light, while the traffic crossing the two other junctions are determined only using yielding rules.

Kvadraturen

The most complex network we consider is a simplified approximation of Kvadraturen in Kristiansand. We only model the roads where we assume there is most traffic, as well as a portion of e18. We consider a total of six different bus lines. The simulation is performed for a total of 750 seconds. In order to get a more interesting minimization problem, we have exaggerated the number of buses travelling through the network.
